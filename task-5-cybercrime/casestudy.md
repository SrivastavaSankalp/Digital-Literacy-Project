# Case Study: The UPI "Golden Rule"
**Cybercrime Type:** UPI / Online Payment Frauds

**What is UPI fraud?**

It is one of the cyber crimes in which scammers deceive people and make them send money through online payment systems like Google Pay or PhonePe. This is done through social engineering, i.e., scammers don't hack the system; they just trick the person.

**For example-** 

1- The victim receives a call from someone claiming to be a bank employee, delivery boy, or customer. 

2- Second the scammers gain the victim’s confidence by talking in a confident tone. 

3- They send a "collect request" through a UPI app and ask the victim to enter their UPI PIN, claiming it will receive money, not send it. Sometimes, they send links or QR codes too. 

4-  When the victim enters their PIN or scans the QR code, the money is immediately transferred to the scammers’ accounts

**Who is targeted?**
Such frauds are common among students, elderly people, or those who are not very aware of online payment systems. People in a hurry or in a state of stress are also potential targets for scammers.

**What are the consequences?**
In UPI frauds, people lose huge amounts of money in just a matter of seconds. It is very hard to recover the lost money.

**Realistic fictional scenario**

Shaurya was called by an individual who claimed to be from his bank, talking in a calm and professional tone. The caller claimed that there was a pending refund of ₹2,500, which needed to be confirmed immediately. Immediately after, Shaurya was sent a UPI collect request, which asked him to enter his PIN to receive the amount. Trusting the individual on the phone, Shaurya entered his PIN, only to realize within a matter of seconds that instead of receiving the refund, ₹2,500 was deducted from his account. When he tried to call back, the phone was switched off, realizing that he had fallen for the scam.
