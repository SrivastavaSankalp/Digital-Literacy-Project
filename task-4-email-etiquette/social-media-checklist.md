# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## ✅ The Do's (What you should do)
- **Think before you post:**   Always consider how your post might be perceived by others, including teachers or future employers. Once posted, content can be permanent.
- **Maintain a professional image:**  Use appropriate language, share achievements, and keep your profile clean, especially on academic or career platforms.
- **Use strong security measures:**  Create strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication to protect your accounts from hacking.
- **Verify information before sharing:**  Cross-check news or posts from reliable sources to avoid spreading misinformation or fake news.
- **Manage privacy settings:**  Regularly review your account settings to control who can see your posts and personal information.

 ## ❌ The Don'ts (What you should avoid)
 - **Don’t share sensitive information:**  Avoid posting personal details like phone numbers, addresses, or financial information publicly.
- **Don’t post offensive or inappropriate content:**  Content that is abusive, controversial, or disrespectful can harm your reputation and relationships.
- **Don’t accept requests from strangers:**  Unknown profiles may be fake or harmful; always verify before connecting.
- **Don’t engage in online conflicts:**  Arguing or trolling can escalate quickly and damage your digital image. Stay respectful and avoid negativity.
- **Don’t overshare your life:**  Posting too much personal content can affect your privacy and may be misused by others.
